ARTICLE OF INCORPORATION
OF
GREATER PURPOSE FOUNDATION
We, the undersigned, under the provisions of the Liberian Business Corporation Act of 1976, Part II, Not–For–Profit Corporations, Chapter 20, to form not-for-profit Corporations, do hereby make, subscribe, acknowledge, and file these Articles of Incorporation to the Liberia Business Registry:


Article I: Name
The name of the Not-for-Profit Corporation (hereinafter known as the ‘Foundation”) shall be Greater Purpose Foundation or GPF.


Article II: Principal Office
The Principal Office of the Foundation shall be located at Joe Bar, Paynesville, Montserrado County,  Liberia, with sub-offices to be established in other counties where the Foundation has programs.

Article IV: Duration
The Foundation shall have perpetual existence.  


Article V: Purpose
The Foundation is established exclusively for charitable, educational, and social development purposes. These purposes include, but are not limited to:
1. Promoting social justice, peace, and gender equality in Liberia and globally.
2. Providing assistance, education, and empowerment to underserved and marginalized communities, particularly focusing on women, children, and people with disabilities.
3. Offering humanitarian aid and support to slum communities, high school students, and at-risk populations.
4. Supporting civic engagement through voter education and democratic processes, including election observation.
5. Advocating for equitable political power distribution and enhanced women's participation in governance and decision-making roles.
No part of the net earnings of the corporation shall benefit any private individual, and the organization shall not engage in activities inconsistent with its non-profit status.


Article VI: Membership
The Foundation shall have no formal membership. The governance of the Foundation shall be vested in its Executives and Board of Directors, whose election and duties shall be outlined in the bylaws.

Article VIII: Non-Profit Status 
The Foundation is organized exclusively for charitable and educational purposes and shall operate as a non-profit entity. No part of its income shall benefit any private individual or shareholder, and it shall not engage in activities that would compromise its tax-exempt status, should it qualify for such under Liberian law or any corresponding section of international non-profit regulations.


Legality
The Foundation shall indemnify any person who is a party to or is threatened to be a party to any completed action, suit, or proceedings, whether criminal, administrative, or investigative (other than an action by or in the right of the Foundation) because the person is or was discharging the duty of another organization, institution and/ or him/herself against (including Attorney’s Fee), judgment, fines and amount paid in settlement actually and reasonably incurred by him in connection with such action, suit, proceedings if to be in or not begin opposed to the best interest of the Foundation.
